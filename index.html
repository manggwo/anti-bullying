The Problem

Forms of Cyberbullying

Online harassment takes many forms. All of them cause real emotional damage to young people.

💬
Harassment
Repeatedly sending offensive, rude, or threatening messages to someone online.
🎭
Impersonation
Creating fake accounts to pretend to be someone else and destroy their reputation.
📸
Image-Based Abuse
Sharing embarrassing or private photos and videos without the person's consent.
🚫
Exclusion
Intentionally leaving someone out of online groups or activities to isolate them.
🔍
Cyberstalking
Monitoring someone's online activity to intimidate, frighten, or control them.

Introduction

Cyberbullying refers to harassment, threats, humiliation, or intimidation that occur through digital platforms such as social media, messaging applications, online gaming, and other internet-based communication tools. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen anytime and anywhere — harmful messages and negative comments can spread quickly, reaching a large audience within seconds.

What makes cyberbullying especially dangerous is its constant presence. Victims may feel there is no escape because online harassment can continue even at home, outside school hours. Prolonged exposure can lead to anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, academic struggles, social withdrawal, and in extreme cases, tragic outcomes.

This project, "Beyond the Screen: When Cyberbullying Becomes Fatal," seeks to emphasize that online actions have real-world consequences. Through awareness campaigns, educational programs, and community engagement, this advocacy aims to reduce incidents of cyberbullying and promote responsible digital citizenship.

Prevention First
Educating students about empathy and digital responsibility reduces harmful incidents before they escalate.
04

Many institutions address bullying only after it occurs. This project focuses on proactive education.

By engaging students, teachers, parents, and sponsors, the campaign creates a unified stand against cyberbullying.
